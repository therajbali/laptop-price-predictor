

## Key Insights
- Airbnb listings are unevenly distributed across neighborhoods, indicating varying levels of competition
- Entire homes/apartments dominate the market, while private rooms represent a smaller but relevant segment
- Availability varies significantly, suggesting different host strategies and regulatory constraints
- Most hosts manage a single listing, while a smaller group operates multiple listings, indicating professional hosting activity

---

## Recommendations
- Hosts should assess neighborhood competition before entering the market
- New hosts may consider private rooms as a lower-competition entry point
- Optimizing availability within regulatory limits may be more effective than maximizing open days
- Single-listing hosts should focus on quality and differentiation rather than scale
